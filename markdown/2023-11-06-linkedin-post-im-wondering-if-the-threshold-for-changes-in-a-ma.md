I'm wondering if the threshold for changes in a mature and stable UI should be:"
"
"1) I am sure I am making it better in the long run"
"2) I am sure because..."
"3) I acknowledge I may be wrong and am willing to look for that possibility actively."
"4) I have thought deeply about making change on my users as gentle as possible.  If really significant, I want to give them a window of time to change when it's hopefully more convenient to them.  (Like "view classic version" banners that feel no shame because they know they are a force for good in the world)"
"5) I have thought to myself, "I know there are some days when a product moves my interface around that I feel frustration"
"6) I have contemplated mortality and the fleeting nature of our place in the world."
"7) I am motivated by the feeling of joy or small satisfaction I am betting my users will get out of this.  Our journey is effort friends but an artist and their art are a conversation on both sides.  Even if one of them costs 99c to see without banners."
"
"
"Imagine if the locks on your windows moved periodically or the location of doors in your rooms every few years."
"
"
"Ok, now let's have a conversation.
